The Alliance's emblem represents the unity forged among the diverse human nations, dwarves, and high elves. The stylized lion symbolizes the strength and nobility of humanity. The lion often holds a hammer, signifying the forging of alliances and the cooperative effort against common threats. The emblem reflects the diverse but united front of the Alliance.

The Alliance

The Alliance is a formidable coalition of noble races.Their story unfolds as they strive to maintain order in the face of emerging dangers, including the undead Scourge and the burning threat of the Orcish Horde. As a beacon of unity and resilience, the Alliance brings together diverse factions, each contributing unique strengths to face the myriad challenges that lie ahead.

Affiliated Factions

Gilneas: Although isolated at times, the human kingdom of Gilneas remains a staunch supporter of the Alliance, contributing skilled warriors and a strong naval presence.

Kul Tiras: As a maritime powerhouse, the island nation of Kul Tiras provides the Alliance with a formidable navy, ensuring control over Azeroth's seas and bolstering the overall strength of the coalition.

Lordaeron Survivors: Despite the fall of Lordaeron to the undead scourge, survivors from this once-great kingdom continue to rally under the Alliance banner, seeking redemption and vengeance against the forces of darkness.

Core Members

Humans (Kingdom of Lordaeron): Led by King Terenas Menethil II, the human kingdom of Lordaeron forms the political center of the Alliance. The fall of Lordaeron to the undead scourge sets the stage for the Alliance's struggle against the insidious forces of the Lich King.

Dwarves (Bronzebeard Clan): The dwarves, staunch allies of the Alliance, contribute their resilience and combat expertise to the cause. Under the leadership of Muradin Bronzebeard, they play a vital role in combating the undead threat.

Elves (High Elves): The High Elves, led by Sylvanas Windrunner, bring their arcane mastery to the Alliance. Their homeland, Quel'Thalas, suffers a devastating blow as the undead scourge, led by the traitor Arthas, seeks to harness the power of the Sunwell.

The Horde's emblem symbolizes the primal and aggressive nature of the orcish clans, trolls, and Tauren. The head of a wolf or an orc is a central motif, representing loyalty, teamwork, and ferocity. The wolf, in particular, is a symbol of the orcs' connection with the natural world and their emphasis on survival and solidarity. The emblem reflects the raw power and unity of the Horde.

The Orcish Horde

The Orcish Horde is a formidable and diverse coalition of orcish clans united under the leadership of Thrall. Driven by a desire for redemption and a new sense of honor, Thrall seeks to carve out a peaceful homeland for his people, far from the conflicts of the Eastern Kingdoms. Through the leadership of Thrall, the Orcish Horde becomes a pivotal force in the struggle against the Burning Legion. ahead.

Affiliated Factions

Tauren (Bloodhoof Tribe): The Tauren, led by Cairne Bloodhoof, form a crucial alliance with the Horde. The Bloodhoof Tribe contributes their strength, resilience, and spiritual connection to nature.

Trolls (Darkspear Tribe): Led by Vol'jin, the Darkspear Tribe plays a significant role in the Horde, providing skilled hunters and warriors. Vol'jin's leadership is vital to the Troll presence within the Horde.

Goblins (Bilgewater Cartel): Goblins from the Bilgewater Cartel ally with the Horde during the events of The Frozen Throne, bringing their technological expertise, cunning, and mercantile skills to the cause.

Ogres (Stonemaul Clan): The Stonemaul Clan, led by Rexxar, consists of ogres who have allied with the Horde. Rexxar's leadership ensures that the ogres contribute their strength and brutality to the Horde's military power.

Core Members

Orcs (Orgrimmar): Thrall's fellow orcs, residing in Orgrimmar, form the backbone of the Horde. Their strength in battle, resilience, and shamanistic traditions contribute significantly to the Horde's military might.

Burning Blade Clan: The Burning Blade Clan, though not directly represented, remains part of the broader orcish culture. Their allegiance may vary, but their presence is acknowledged.

Blackrock Clan: The Blackrock Clan, a prominent orcish clan, has a historical significance. While not directly involved in the events of the campaigns, their influence is part of the orcish heritage.

Warsong Clan: The Warsong Clan, known for its warrior traditions, played a significant role in orcish history. Although not directly featured, their legacy influences the Horde's ethos.

Frostwolf Clan: The Frostwolf Clan, led by Durotan, is integral to orcish history. While not directly involved in the campaigns, their honor and resilience shape the Horde's identity.

The Night Elves' emblem draws inspiration from their close connection to nature and the goddess Elune. Elements such as leaves, trees, and crescent moons are common. The crescent moon, associated with Elune, symbolizes the Night Elves' affinity for the night and their guardianship of nature. The emblem embodies their harmonious relationship with the natural world, emphasizing their role as protectors.

The Night Elves

The Night Elves, an ancient and mystical race, inhabit the dense forests of Kalimdor. Led by the enigmatic Tyrande Whisperwind and her companion Malfurion Stormrage, the Night Elves are deeply connected to nature and the Well of Eternity. As they grapple with the intrusion of outside forces, including the Burning Legion and the tensions within their own society, the Night Elves seek to preserve the sanctity of their ancestral lands and uphold the balance between nature and civilization.

Affiliated Factions

Dryads (Sentinels): The Sentinels, led by Tyrande, are an elite group of Night Elf warriors, archers, and spellcasters. They act as guardians of the Night Elf lands and contribute to the defense of Kalimdor.

Keepers of the Grove (Cenarion Circle): The Cenarion Circle, led by Malfurion Stormrage, includes druids and protectors dedicated to maintaining the balance of nature. They work closely with the Night Elves to safeguard the forests and natural order.

Highborne (Shen'dralar): The Shen'dralar, once part of the Highborne caste, seek redemption and reconciliation with the Night Elves. Although not directly featured in the campaigns, their presence is acknowledged in Night Elf society.

Core Members

Night Elves (Kalimdor Empire): The Night Elves, as a whole, are led by the wise and fierce Tyrande Whisperwind and the powerful Archdruid Malfurion Stormrage. Their ancient civilization, steeped in tradition and magic, forms the core of the Night Elf society.

Dryads (Sentinels): The Sentinels, under Tyrande's leadership, are elite warriors dedicated to protecting Night Elf territories. Their combat skills and loyalty make them essential to the Night Elf forces.

Keepers of the Grove (Cenarion Circle): The druids of the Cenarion Circle, led by Malfurion Stormrage, represent the heart of Night Elf nature magic. Malfurion's wisdom and power are crucial in the defense of Kalimdor.

Ancient Protectors (World Tree Guardians): The Ancient Protectors, ancient tree guardians, serve as the Night Elves' formidable defenders. These mighty beings contribute to the Night Elves' defensive strategies and play a key role in battles.

Wisp Spirits: The Wisp Spirits, ethereal entities tied to Night Elf magic, provide unique support to the Night Elves. They play a role in construction, scouting, and even combat, showcasing the Night Elves' mystical connection to nature.

The Scourge's emblem reflects their dark and necromantic nature. Featuring skulls, bones, and morbid symbols, it represents death, decay, and the undead. The sinister imagery symbolizes the Scourge's allegiance to the Lich King and their role as agents of the Burning Legion in spreading undeath across Azeroth. The emblem embodies the terror and malevolence associated with the undead.

The Undead Scourge

The Undead Scourge, a relentless force led by the menacing Lich King, Arthas Menethil, seeks to spread death and darkness across Azeroth. Comprising undead minions, the Scourge is fueled by the necromantic power of the Lich King and the cunning strategies of figures like Kel'Thuzad and Sylvanas Windrunner. As the Scourge advances, it leaves a trail of devastation, converting the living into loyal minions in its quest for dominion over the world. The Undead Scourge's chilling presence poses a grave threat to all life on Azeroth.

Affiliated Factions

Cult of the Damned: The Cult of the Damned, led by the necromancer Kel'Thuzad, serves as a key faction within the Scourge. They play a significant role in spreading the undead plague and carrying out the will of the Lich King.

Banshee's Veil (Forsaken): The Forsaken, under the leadership of Sylvanas Windrunner, break free from the Lich King's control. As the Banshee's Veil, they form a faction within the Scourge but later establish their own identity and goals.

Dreadlords: The Dreadlords, such as Tichondrius, are powerful demon commanders serving the Burning Legion. While aligned with the Scourge, their ultimate allegiance lies with the Legion's goals.

Core Members

Lich King (Ner'zhul/Arthas): The Lich King, initially Ner'zhul and later merged with Arthas Menethil, is the central figure leading the Undead Scourge. His dark powers and influence drive the Scourge's relentless campaign across Azeroth.

Dark Lady Sylvanas Windrunner: Sylvanas Windrunner, once a high elf ranger-general, breaks free from the Lich King's control and becomes the Dark Lady of the Forsaken. Her leadership shapes the destiny of the undead and leads to the establishment of the Forsaken faction.

Kel'Thuzad: Kel'Thuzad, a powerful necromancer, serves as a key lieutenant of the Lich King. His dark magic and cunning strategies contribute to the Scourge's malevolent plans, especially during the events of Warcraft III.

Dreadlords (Tichondrius): Tichondrius and other Dreadlords act as powerful commanders within the Scourge, serving both the Lich King and their demonic masters in the Burning Legion.

Abominations and Crypt Fiends: Undead creatures like Abominations and Crypt Fiends serve as core units in the Scourge's armies, embodying the twisted creations of necromantic magic.
